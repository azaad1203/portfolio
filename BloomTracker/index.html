    Which Eucalyptus Should I Pick?

    75% of honey production in Australia is derived from the Eucalyptus species. The following species are ranked as the most important nectar plants in Victoria by beekeepers, and thus are the species we have chosen for our prediction model.

    Grey Box

    The Grey Box Eucalyptus (eucalyptus microcarpa) is the most commonly occurring species of Eucalypt, and reliably produces nectar and pollen every year. Honey from Grey Box is usually a medium to dark amber colour.

    Red Gum

    Named for ruby coloured timber, Red Gum (eucalyptus camuldulensis) is the most widespread species of Eucalyptus in Australia. The honey from Red Gum is a golden colour with a mild, smooth flavour.

    Yellow Gum

    The Yellow Gum can be found across South Australia and Victoria. Flowers may be white, cream, pink or red. The honey from the Yellow Gum (eucalyptus leucoxylon) is pale yellow in colour, very dense with a strong flavour.

    Yellow Box

    The flowers of the Yellow Box Eucalyptus are white in colour.The taste from the Yellow Box (eucalyptus melliodora) honey is likely to be what most Australians think of when they think of honey. It is a pale straw colour and quite aromatic.

    Messmate

    Eucalyptus obliqua is also known as Australian Oak or Brown Top, and is native to south-eastern Australia. The honey produced is dark in colour and has a strong pronounced flavour.
